Common Issues

Connection Failed

If GuardBar cannot connect to your AdGuard Home server:

  • Verify your AdGuard Home server is running and accessible
  • Check that the host/IP address and port are correct in Settings
  • Ensure your username and password are correct
  • Try accessing your AdGuard Home dashboard directly in a browser: http://your-server:port
  • Check if your Mac and AdGuard Home server are on the same network (or reachable)

Local Network Permission

macOS may prompt you to allow GuardBar to access devices on your local network. This is required for GuardBar to communicate with your AdGuard Home server.

  • Click "Allow" when prompted
  • If you denied permission, go to System Settings → Privacy & Security → Local Network
  • Enable GuardBar in the list

Status Not Updating

If the protection status seems stuck or not updating:

  • Click the menu bar icon and use the "Refresh" button
  • Check your polling interval in Settings (Preferences tab)
  • Verify the connection to your AdGuard Home server is working

Timer Not Working

If the auto re-enable timer isn't working as expected:

  • Check that your Mac isn't going to sleep during the timer period
  • Verify the timer is still active by opening the menu bar dropdown
  • You can cancel an active timer and manually re-enable protection

System Requirements

  • macOS 14.0 (Sonoma) or later
  • AdGuard Home instance running on your network
  • AdGuard Home credentials (username and password)

Privacy

GuardBar does not collect any user data. All settings are stored locally on your Mac. For more information, see our Privacy Policy.
