Privacy Policy for GuardBar

Last Updated: November 12, 2024

Overview

GuardBar is a macOS menu bar application that helps you manage your AdGuard Home instance. Your privacy is important to us, and this policy explains how GuardBar handles your data.

GuardBar does not collect, transmit, or store any user data on external servers.

All data remains local on your Mac.

Information Stored Locally

GuardBar stores the following information locally on your device:

Connection Settings

  • AdGuard Home server address (host/IP and port)
  • Username
  • Password (stored securely in macOS Keychain)

These settings are stored using macOS UserDefaults and Keychain, and never leave your device.

App Preferences

  • Polling interval settings
  • Timer preset preferences
  • Start at login preference
  • Notification preferences

Network Communication

GuardBar communicates directly with your AdGuard Home server using the connection details you provide. This communication:

  • Occurs only between your Mac and your AdGuard Home server
  • Uses HTTP Basic Authentication
  • Is not routed through any third-party servers
  • Is not monitored, logged, or stored by the developer

Third-Party Services

GuardBar does not use any third-party analytics, tracking, or advertising services.

Data Sharing

GuardBar does not share any data with third parties. All communication is between your Mac and your AdGuard Home server.

Security

  • Passwords are stored securely in the macOS Keychain
  • All communication uses your specified connection method (HTTP/HTTPS)
  • No data is transmitted to the developer or any third party

Children's Privacy

GuardBar does not knowingly collect any information from children.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected in the app's documentation and on the GitHub repository.
